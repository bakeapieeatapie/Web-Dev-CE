Who Were The Signers?

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Main author of the Declaration. Later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A strong advocate for independence. Later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
A respected elder statesman, scientist, and diplomat. Played a key role in gaining French support during the Revolution.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress at the time. Known for his bold, large signature — “John Hancock” became slang for a signature.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Cousin of John Adams. One of the key organizers of resistance against British policies, including the Boston Tea Party.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign all four major founding documents of the U.S.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Key financier of the American Revolution.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration and the last surviving one (died in 1832).
